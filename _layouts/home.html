Stefan's Story

I was born in Austin and grew up in San Francisco. At age 18 I traded the checkered Vans of a bodysurfer for the leather chukka boots of a Yale student and spent the next decade in New Haven and New York City before moving to Austin, where I live today.

  • beginnings

    A love of chemistry

    Like Oliver Sacks, my first love was chemistry. As a child I hung out at the Lawrence Hall of Science, collected serpentine crystals in the California hills, and hunted for fossils and geodes in Nevada.

  • education

    Yale University and The University of Texas at Austin

    At Yale I studied everything I could: art history, literature, classics, philosophy, neurophysiology... Several courses by Heinrich von Staden were influential and gave me a chance to read Ovid and Galen and gain insights into the interplay between philosophy, technology, and medicine.

  • Teaching

    Undergraduate seminars in Science, Technology, and Society Department

    The seminars explored the origins of counting, writing, and computing, and introduced the principles of human-computer interaction. Students were asked to reinvent and then use pivotal technologies: Renaissance-era perspective machines and Turing machines made of toilet paper, a die, and post-it notes.

  • Today

    Product management and product design

    For over fifteen years I've been product managing and designing software for companies large and small. I'm especially proud of my years at Google, where I worked on Geo-Targeting and Docs.
